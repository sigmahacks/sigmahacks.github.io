About The Hackathon

SigmaHacks is a high school hackathon organized by Cupertino High School students. Hundreds of high schoolers from across the Bay Area come together in one 28-hour coding event to turn their ideas into reality. Throughout the weekend, teams of up to 4 people will work together to create innovative solutions to real-world problems. We will be providing food, mentors, workshops, games, swag, and more food, all for free! No matter whether you've never coded before or this is your 20th hackathon, be sure not to miss this event!

Our mission is to expose more teens to STEM fields in an exciting, interactive way. In addition, we aim to make girls more comfortable collaborating with others in tech fields and more confident in their skills in order to decrease the gender gap.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a weekend-long coding event where you turn your crazy ideas into real-world projects. We provide food, swag, mentors, workshops, guest speakers, hardware, and so much more! You do your hacking, and we’ll do the rest.

Who can attend?

SigmaHacks is open to all high schoolers. If you have graduated from high school, you can apply to be a mentor. Unfortunately, we are not accepting middle school participants at this time.

What if I don’t know how to code?

Not a problem at all! SigmaHacks is the ideal place to learn how to code, and we’ll have plenty of mentors and workshops to help you along the way.

What if I don’t have a team or idea?

Don’t worry! At the beginning of the hackathon, swe’ll have team forming activities, along with workshops for brainstorming ideas!

How much does it cost?

SigmaHacks is a 100% free for all hackers. We provide food, drinks, swag, WiFi, prizes, and more, all free of charge.

What should I bring?

Hackers should bring a laptop, and a a valid ID (or some sort of student ID). We also recommend that you bring your phone, chargers, power strips, toiletries, a sleeping bag, and any other supplies you might need.

Do I need to stay overnight?

No; although we highly recommend staying for the entire duration of the hackathon. Doors will be closed from 10pm to 7am, and you will have to be signed out by a legal guardian.

What is the code of conduct?

We abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.
